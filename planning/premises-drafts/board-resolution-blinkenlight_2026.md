# BLINKENLIGHT Ltd

## Minutes of a meeting of the board of directors

**Company:** BLINKENLIGHT Ltd (company no. 13110356)

**Present:** Patrick Coleman (director); Vytautas Rudys (director)

### 1. Chair and quorum

Patrick Coleman was appointed chair of the meeting. The chair noted that the
meeting had been duly convened, that due notice had been given to all the
directors, and that a quorum was present (see minute 3 as to the participation
of the interested directors). The chair declared the meeting open.

### 2. Purpose

The purpose of the meeting was to consider, and if thought fit approve, the
company entering into the Co-occupation and Shared-Facilities Agreement between
the company and Pemberton Digital Ltd (the "Agreement"), under which the
company grants Pemberton a non-exclusive licence to occupy and use space at the
company's premises at Unit 309 The Pill Box, together with shared use of
laboratory equipment and facilities.

### 3. Declarations of interest and authority to participate

In accordance with section 177 of the Companies Act 2006 (CA 2006) and the
company's articles of association, the directors declared their interests in
the Agreement:

- Patrick Coleman declared that he is also a director and a significant
  shareholder of Pemberton Digital Ltd; and
- Vytautas Rudys declared that he is also a minority shareholder of Pemberton
  Digital Ltd.

It was noted that the company has adopted the model articles for private
companies limited by shares and that, pursuant to article 14(3)(a) of those
articles, the members had by written ordinary resolution dated
`TODO(patrick): date of members' resolution` disapplied the restriction in
article 14 in respect of the Agreement, so that each director, having declared
his interest, was entitled to be counted in the quorum and to vote on the
resolutions below.

### 4. The Agreement

A copy of the Agreement was tabled. The directors reviewed its principal terms
and noted that:

- it formalises a co-occupation arrangement that has been in place since
  October 2022;
- it grants Pemberton a non-exclusive, month-to-month licence terminable on
  14 days' written notice;
- Pemberton pays £876.04 per calendar month plus VAT, representing
  approximately 50% of the company's own head occupation cost for the premises
  (evidenced by the head occupation agreement held on file) and a fair,
  market-related share of the facility costs the company bears;
- equipment ownership is preserved by asset-tagging, each party maintains and
  insures its own equipment, and the parties give mutual confidentiality and
  business-scope and intellectual-property acknowledgements; and
- although the parties are related, the terms are intended to be on an
  arm's-length, market-related basis.

### 5. Resolutions

The directors, having regard to the matters set out above, considered that
entering into the Agreement promotes the success of the company for the benefit
of its members as a whole (section 172 CA 2006). After due consideration, IT
WAS RESOLVED THAT:

(a) the company enter into the Agreement substantially in the form tabled; and

(b) Vytautas Rudys be and is authorised to sign the Agreement on behalf of the
company, and to agree and initial any minor amendments to it.

The resolutions were passed unanimously.

### 6. Close

There being no further business, the chair declared the meeting closed.
