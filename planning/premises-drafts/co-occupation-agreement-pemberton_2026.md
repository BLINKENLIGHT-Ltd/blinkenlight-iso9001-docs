# Co-occupation and Shared-Facilities Agreement

**Between:**

1. **BLINKENLIGHT Ltd** (company no. 13110356), registered office Unit 309
   The Pill Box, 115 Coventry Road, London E2 6GH ("BLINKENLIGHT"); and
2. **Pemberton Digital Ltd** (company no. 14422623), registered office
   Lytchett House, 13 Freeland Park, Wareham Road, Poole, Dorset BH16 6FA
   ("Pemberton").

Each a "party" and together the "parties".

---

### 1. Purpose and mutual benefit

The parties have shared premises since Pemberton was founded in October
2022, and currently share the premises and laboratory at Unit 309 The Pill
Box, 115 Coventry Road, London E2 6GH (the "premises"). Co-locating benefits
both companies: it reduces each party's facility cost, gives each access
to a wider range of test and measurement equipment than either would
maintain alone, and allows shared use of tools and other resources. This
agreement records, in good faith, the practical terms on which the two
companies co-exist at the premises.

### 2. Relationship between the parties

The parties are under common control: Patrick Coleman is a director and a
significant shareholder of both companies. This agreement is entered into
between parties under common control; although they are related, its terms —
including the charge in clause 3.4 — are intended to be fair and on a
market-related basis. It is set down so that the basis on which the companies
share the premises, equipment and resources is clear and on the record.

### 3. Licence to occupy

3.1 BLINKENLIGHT grants Pemberton a non-exclusive licence to occupy and use
space at the premises. The licence does not create a tenancy or any estate
or interest in the premises; it is personal to Pemberton.

3.2 The licence runs on a calendar month-to-month basis. Either party may
end it by giving the other 14 days' written notice.

3.3 The licence includes:

- access to and use of the licensed space;
- access to and use of the laboratory and test equipment owned by
  BLINKENLIGHT;
- storage of Pemberton's items at the premises;
- electrical power and heating;
- internet access;
- refuse collection; and
- handling of Pemberton's mail.

3.4 Pemberton pays BLINKENLIGHT **£876.04 per calendar month plus VAT** for
the licence and the included services.

3.5 The current premises and the charge in clause 3.4 have applied since
17 April 2026. The parties' shared occupation itself dates from October 2022
(clause 1).

### 4. Equipment and asset ownership

4.1 Each party marks the equipment and other tangible items it owns with its
own asset tags: BLINKENLIGHT items carry BLINKENLIGHT asset tags, Pemberton
items carry Pemberton asset tags.

4.2 Asset tagging records ownership. The presence of an item at the shared
premises does not transfer its ownership to the other party.

4.3 Each party is responsible for the maintenance, calibration where
applicable, and insurance of its own equipment.

4.4 Each party takes reasonable care of the other party's items kept at the
premises.

### 5. Mutual confidentiality

5.1 Each party may, through co-occupation, receive confidential information
belonging to the other — including business, technical, commercial and
customer information, and confidential information belonging to the other
party's customers or other third parties.

5.2 Each party will keep the other's confidential information confidential;
will protect it using at least the measures it uses for its own confidential
information of like kind, and in any event no less than reasonable measures;
will use it only as needed for the shared use of the premises; will disclose it
only to those of its directors, employees and contractors who need to know it
for that purpose; and will not disclose it to any other third party without the
other party's consent.

5.3 This clause does not apply to information that: is or becomes public
through no fault of the receiving party; was already known to the receiving
party before disclosure; is independently developed by the receiving party
without use of the other party's confidential information; or that a party is
required to disclose by law. It continues to apply after this agreement ends.

5.4 On termination of this agreement, or on the other party's written request,
each party will return or securely destroy the other party's confidential
information in its possession or control, save for copies it is required to
retain by law or that exist in routine electronic backups.

### 6. Business scope and intellectual property

6.1 BLINKENLIGHT acknowledges that Pemberton's business is business
intelligence products, sensors and services — including people counting, queue
management, compliance monitoring and stock-level monitoring, and related
consulting services — in the retail and hospitality sectors (the "Pemberton
Fields"); including, for example, the CF-10 and KC-10 advanced analytics
sensors and the Pemberton analytics dashboard. BLINKENLIGHT confirms that it
carries on no business in the Pemberton Fields and that it neither holds nor
claims any intellectual property rights in the Pemberton Fields, whenever and
by whomever created.

6.2 Pemberton acknowledges that BLINKENLIGHT's business is radio-frequency
(RF) design, manufacture and consulting; antenna design, manufacture and
consulting; and electronics and product design, manufacture and consulting
(the "BLINKENLIGHT Fields"). Other than the electronics embodied in its own
products described in clause 6.1, Pemberton confirms that it carries on no
business in the BLINKENLIGHT Fields and that it neither holds nor claims any
intellectual property rights in the BLINKENLIGHT Fields, whenever and by
whomever created.

6.3 The parties' co-occupation, and any shared use of the premises,
equipment, tools or resources, does not give either party any right, title or
interest in intellectual property belonging to the other party, or to any
director, officer, employee or contractor of the other party, in the
BLINKENLIGHT Fields or the Pemberton Fields. Nothing in this agreement
transfers, assigns or licenses any intellectual property between the parties.

### 7. Liability

7.1 Each party is responsible to the other for loss of or damage to the other
party's property to the extent caused by its own negligence or by its breach of
this agreement. This is in addition to each party's duty to take reasonable
care of the other's items under clause 4.4.

7.2 Subject to clause 7.3, neither party is liable to the other for any
indirect or consequential loss, or for any loss of profit, revenue, business,
goodwill or data, whether arising in contract, tort (including negligence) or
otherwise.

7.3 Nothing in this agreement excludes or limits either party's liability for:
death or personal injury caused by its negligence; fraud or fraudulent
misrepresentation; or any other liability that cannot lawfully be excluded or
limited.

7.4 Each party is responsible for insuring its own property and equipment (see
clause 4.3).

7.5 Subject to clause 7.3, and except in respect of the confidentiality
obligations in clause 5 and the intellectual-property provisions in clause 6
(to which no cap applies), each party's total aggregate liability to the other
arising out of or in connection with this agreement, whether in contract, tort
(including negligence) or otherwise, is limited to an amount equal to the
licence fee payable under clause 3.4 for one year — that is, twelve times the
monthly charge (currently £10,512.48).

### 8. Status, term and general

8.1 The parties have co-existed at shared premises since Pemberton was
founded in October 2022. This agreement records and formalises that
arrangement as it currently stands; the current premises and the charge apply
from 17 April 2026 (clause 3.5). The agreement continues until the licence is
ended under clause 3.2. The confidentiality and intellectual-property
acknowledgements in clauses 5 and 6 survive its end. The date shown beside each
signature is the date that party signed.

8.2 The parties intend the licence and charge in clause 3, the equipment and
ownership provisions in clause 4, the confidentiality provisions in clause 5,
the business-scope and intellectual-property acknowledgements in clause 6, and
the liability provisions in clause 7 to be binding; the remainder records the
parties' good-faith understanding.

8.3 This agreement sets out the parties' entire understanding on the matters
it covers and supersedes any previous informal arrangement between them on
those matters.

8.4 This agreement may be signed in counterpart and by electronic signature.

8.5 This agreement is governed by the law of England and Wales.
