# Pemberton Digital Ltd

## Minutes of a meeting of the board of directors

**Company:** Pemberton Digital Ltd (company no. 14422623)

**Present:** Andrew Cameron (director); Patrick Coleman (director)

### 1. Chair and quorum

Andrew Cameron was appointed chair of the meeting. The chair noted that the
meeting had been duly convened, that due notice had been given to all the
directors, and that a quorum was present. The chair declared the meeting open.

### 2. Purpose

The purpose of the meeting was to consider, and if thought fit approve, the
company entering into the Co-occupation and Shared-Facilities Agreement between
the company and BLINKENLIGHT Ltd (the "Agreement"), under which BLINKENLIGHT
grants the company a non-exclusive licence to occupy and use space at Unit 309
The Pill Box, 115 Coventry Road, London E2 6GH, together with shared use of
laboratory equipment and facilities.

### 3. Declaration of interest and authority to participate

In accordance with section 177 of the Companies Act 2006 (CA 2006) and the
company's articles of association, Patrick Coleman declared the nature and
extent of his interest in the Agreement, being also a director and a
significant shareholder of BLINKENLIGHT Ltd.

It was noted that, the company having disapplied model articles 13 and 14, and
in accordance with articles 28.5 and 29.8 of the company's articles of
association, Patrick Coleman — having declared his interest — was entitled to
be counted in the quorum and to vote on the resolutions below.

### 4. Connected-party transaction — approval under the Shareholders Agreement

The board noted that BLINKENLIGHT Ltd is a connected person of a director and
shareholder of the company, and that entering the Agreement is therefore a
transaction within Schedule 6, Part 2, paragraph 7 of the Subscription and
Shareholders Agreement dated 12 November 2023 (the "SHA"), requiring Board
Approval. The board confirmed that the resolutions below constitute that Board
Approval. The board further noted that:

- the value of the arrangement (£876.04 per calendar month plus VAT) is below
  the £25,000 threshold for capital expenditure and leasing arrangements in
  Schedule 6, Part 2, paragraph 4, and does not constitute indebtedness within
  Schedule 6, Part 1, so no Investor Majority Consent is required;
- the terms are on an arm's-length, market-related basis; and
- the arrangement will be disclosed to the Investors through the company's
  information-rights reporting.

### 5. The Agreement

A copy of the Agreement was tabled. The directors reviewed its principal terms
and noted that:

- it formalises a co-occupation arrangement that has been in place since the
  company's founding in October 2022;
- it grants the company a non-exclusive, month-to-month licence terminable on
  14 days' written notice, including access to and use of BLINKENLIGHT's
  laboratory equipment, storage, power, heating, internet, refuse collection
  and mail handling;
- the charge of £876.04 per calendar month plus VAT represents approximately
  50% of BLINKENLIGHT's head occupation cost for the premises and a fair,
  market-related share of the shared facility costs; and
- the parties give mutual confidentiality and business-scope and
  intellectual-property acknowledgements.

### 6. Resolutions

The directors, having regard to the matters set out above, considered that
entering into the Agreement promotes the success of the company for the benefit
of its members as a whole (section 172 CA 2006). After due consideration, IT
WAS RESOLVED THAT:

(a) the company enter into the Agreement substantially in the form tabled, and
that this approval constitutes the Board Approval required under Schedule 6,
Part 2, paragraph 7 of the SHA; and

(b) Andrew Cameron be and is authorised to sign the Agreement on behalf of the
company.

The resolutions were passed with the concurrence of Andrew Cameron as the
disinterested director.

### 7. Close

There being no further business, the chair declared the meeting closed.
