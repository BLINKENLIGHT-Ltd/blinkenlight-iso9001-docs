# UKAS-accredited ISO 9001:2015 certification bodies — shortlist for BLINKENLIGHT Ltd

Research note feeding Phase 2 of `PLAN.md`. Output is a shortlist to
quote and a quotes-checklist. Final selection is Patrick's.

## Method

Verification of UKAS (United Kingdom Accreditation Service)
accreditation must be performed directly against the UKAS
accredited-organisations search before any quote is requested. Do not
rely on the body's own marketing.

- Search interface: https://www.ukas.com/find-an-organisation/
- Select "Certification Bodies" as the organisation type.
- Verify each shortlisted body's accreditation schedule explicitly
  lists ISO 9001:2015 and that the scope covers electronics /
  engineering services.
- Certificate verification (post-issue): https://certcheck.ukas.com

The shortlist below was assembled from sources that frequently
recommend these bodies for UK SMEs (Small and Medium-sized
Enterprises) and micro businesses. Each must still be verified against
UKAS before quoting.

## Shortlist for BLINKENLIGHT Ltd

Recommended bodies to quote, in order of fit for a solo UK
RF/electronics consultancy with aerospace-adjacent customers:

### 1. NQA (National Quality Assurance)

- Website: https://www.nqa.com/en-gb/
- Reputation: widely cited as SME-friendly. UKAS-accredited and also
  accredited by ANAB (ANSI National Accreditation Board) in the
  United States — useful if any future buyer requires US recognition.
- Audit style: streamlined; reasonable cost; remote-audit policy
  exists for surveillance audits.
- Sector coverage: broad. Used widely by engineering and electronics
  firms.
- Verify before quote: https://www.ukas.com/find-an-organisation/

### 2. Alcumus ISOQAR

- Website: https://www.alcumus.com/en-gb/isoqar/
- Reputation: also frequently recommended for UK SMEs. UKAS-
  accredited.
- Audit style: cost-competitive against NQA; broad scope coverage.
- Verify before quote: https://www.ukas.com/find-an-organisation/

### 3. British Assessment Bureau (Amtivo)

- Website: https://amtivo.com/uk/
- Reputation: positions explicitly toward smaller businesses. Now
  trading as Amtivo following acquisition; verify the UKAS schedule
  carries the current legal entity name.
- Audit style: SME-focused; published indicative pricing aimed at
  small firms.
- Verify before quote: https://www.ukas.com/find-an-organisation/

### Bodies considered and de-prioritised (for now)

- **BSI (British Standards Institution).** Heavyweight, broadest UKAS
  scope of any UK certification body, name carries weight in tenders.
  De-prioritised on cost-to-value grounds for a solo: audit style is
  thorough and fees sit at the upper end. Revisit only if a target
  buyer (e.g. an MoD (Ministry of Defence) prime) specifically asks
  for BSI.
- **URS (United Registrar of Systems), QMS International, Citation
  ISO, SGS.** Worth a verification pass on UKAS if the top-three
  shortlist quotes come back unattractive; not the starting point.

### Out of scope

- Any body that is not UKAS-accredited for ISO 9001:2015, regardless
  of price. The credibility-stack goal makes a non-accredited
  certificate worse than none — see the lost-deal diagnosis in
  `~/claude-workspace/blinkenlight/research/00-positioning-options.md`.
- Any body that bundles the consultancy that drafts the QMS with the
  audit that certifies it. ISO/IEC 17021-1 (the standard UKAS enforces
  on certification bodies) treats this as a threat to impartiality and
  prohibits it. The boundary is real; ignore practitioners who claim
  otherwise.

## Quotes checklist — to request from each shortlisted body

Include in each request for quotation:

1. **Confirmation of UKAS accreditation** for ISO 9001:2015 covering
   electronics design and development as a scope of supply. Reference
   the body's UKAS schedule.
2. **Initial certification cost** (Stage 1 + Stage 2 combined),
   itemised by audit days.
3. **Annual surveillance audit cost** in Years 1 and 2.
4. **Recertification audit cost** in Year 3.
5. **Total 3-year cost** stated as a single figure.
6. **Audit-day count** for a one-person, one-site, low-risk operation
   (the cert body will compute this from the UKAS sector formula —
   ask for the computation and the inputs).
7. **Remote-audit policy** for surveillance years. On-site is
   expected for Stage 1 and Stage 2.
8. **Lead auditor profile** typically assigned to electronics /
   aerospace-adjacent scope.
9. **Travel and expenses** policy and whether they are included in the
   quoted price.
10. **Brand-mark and certificate logo** usage rules and any cost.
11. **Notice required** to book Stage 1 and Stage 2 from a
    quote-accepted state.
12. **Cancellation and rescheduling** terms (especially for surveillance).
13. **Notification of changes to the audit schedule from their side**
    and how rebookings are handled.
14. **Familiarity** with Git-rendered markdown QMS (some lead auditors
    will be more comfortable with this than others; the rendered
    Audit Pack PDFs are intended to remove any friction, but it is
    worth asking).

## Indicative cost frame

From the search literature, a realistic Year-1 envelope for a UK
solo, one-site, low-risk consultancy:

| Line item                                  | Indicative range |
|--------------------------------------------|------------------|
| UKAS initial assessment (Stage 1 + Stage 2)| £1,400 – £3,500  |
| Year-1 surveillance audit                  | £750 – £1,500    |
| Year-2 surveillance audit                  | £750 – £1,500    |
| Year-3 recertification audit               | £1,400 – £3,500  |
| **3-year UKAS audit total**                | **~£4,300 – £10,000** |

Excludes:
- External internal auditor (see `PLAN.md` Decision 2b; ~£600–£1,500
  per year ongoing).
- Calibration provider (see `PLAN.md` Decision 2c; ~£500–£2,000 per
  year ongoing).
- Standards purchase (BS EN ISO 9001:2015 PDF, optional companion
  BS EN ISO/TS 9002:2016) — ~£200 one-off via BSI Shop.

Note: consultant fees are excluded by choice. The plan does not use
an external implementation consultant; AI-maintained drafting plus a
gap-analysis-led approach handles the work that a paid consultant
would otherwise do.
